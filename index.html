Dave's Case

His 3 biggest warnings.
Stated fairly.

He's helped millions of people. His arguments deserve the strongest version of themselves.

WARNING 01
"You will overspend."
Dave's Case

Paying with plastic doesn't hurt the way handing over cash does. Your brain doesn't register the loss the same way. Studies back this up — people consistently spend more with cards than with cash. For the 71% with no budget? He's completely right.

For the 29%

If you have a budget, swiping $400 on groceries feels exactly like handing over $400 cash — because you already know that money is gone. The problem was never the card. It was the absence of a system.

WARNING 02
"High interest will trap you."
Dave's Case

Credit card APRs run 20–22% right now. It starts with one tight month, one emergency. The balance creeps up. Interest compounds. Suddenly you're paying interest on interest and the hole gets deeper every month. This is real and it destroys people financially.

For the 29%

The interest rate is completely irrelevant if you never carry a balance. Auto-pay, full balance, every month. The APR becomes a number that literally never touches you. It's like warning someone about drunk driving when they don't drink.

WARNING 03
"You don't need a credit score — and rewards aren't worth it."
Dave's Case

On scores — you can get a mortgage through manual underwriting without a credit score. Technically true. On rewards — his team studied 10,167 millionaires. Zero said they got wealthy from airline miles. Also true.

For the 29%

"Technically possible" is doing a lot of heavy lifting. No score means invisible — and invisible is expensive. Worse rates, fewer lenders, higher PMI, harder apartment approvals. On rewards: nobody said you get rich from miles. But $1,200/year on spend you're already making, over 10 years, is $12,000. That's not wealth. That's just not leaving free money on the table.

"All three warnings are real. All three collapse if you have a budget and the discipline to stick to it."

What Actually Matters

5 FICO factors.
Forget 3 of them.

For a disciplined player, only two drive your score. Everything else is noise.

01
Credit Age
The only variable you can't hack.

How long your oldest account has been open. No shortcut. No app. No hustle. Just time passing — and it compounds automatically, forever, as long as the account stays open.

"You can always decide not to use credit cards tomorrow. You cannot decide to have started 5 years ago."

Every year you wait is a year you can never recover. Open beginner cards early. Keep them open forever.
02
On-Time Payments
The one that can nuke you overnight.

One 30-day late payment can drop your score 80+ points. It stays on your report for 7 years.

The fix: Set up auto-pay for the full balance the day the card arrives. 60 seconds. Done forever.

This is the highest-ROI 60 seconds you'll spend on your finances this year. Auto-pay. Full balance. Day one.
